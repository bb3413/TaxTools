California Estimated Tax Calculator Help

This is a tool for estimating California income tax. It is intended to help you determine how much California state income tax you would owe given the data you provide. The California income tax is based on the results of the federal tax computation, so the value for many of the fields can be found on the federal return.

The California income tax calculation starts with the federal adjusted gross income (AGI). Then, for each difference between the federal and California tax law an adjustment is either added or subtracted from the federal AGI. On the California tax return form 540, these adjustments are aggregated into Subtractions on line 14 and Additions on line 16.

In the input fields in this tool, items that need to be added or subtracted from the federal AGI will be listed in the corresponding section "Subtractions from Federal AGI" or "Additions to Federal AGI". However, in the actual calculation of the California tax return, these items may not end up in the Subtractions on line 14 or Additions on line 16 that you expect.

While income items always go to the Addition or Subtractions based on whether they will be added or subtracted from the federal AGI, adjustments to income do not. For example, an adjustment that will increase the federal AGI, may be subtracted from the subtractions rather than added to the additions. And the reverse may be true as well.

In this tool, the fields where you enter data are organized by their effect on the federal AGI, but the Subtractions and Additions categories shown in the estimated tax calculation will be calculated as they are on the California tax return.

The tax formula for calculating the California income tax is also provided to show, at a high level, how the tax is calculated. The value of each item in the tax formula is shown in the "Estimated Tax Calculation" section.
